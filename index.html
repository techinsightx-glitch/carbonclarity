Why Carbon Footprint Matters

Greenhouse gases such as CO₂ trap heat in the atmosphere, contributing to global warming — the long-term rise in Earth's average temperature. This warming results in more extreme weather, rising sea levels, and ecosystem disruption.

By understanding your carbon footprint — the total greenhouse gas emissions caused directly or indirectly by your actions — you can take steps to reduce your personal impact.

Actions like using renewable energy, reducing car travel, minimizing flights, managing waste effectively, and eating fewer meat meals contribute substantially to lowering emissions. Every ton of CO₂ you reduce helps slow down climate change and protects the planet for future generations.
